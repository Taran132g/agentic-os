Something happens that should make you happy... and you feel almost nothing. And a quiet part of you starts wondering if something in you is broken.
It's not. You're not empty. You're guarded.
Somewhere it got safer to feel nothing than to feel the hard stuff, so you turned the volume down on all of it. The problem is that dial doesn't only lower the pain. It lowers the good too.
So the wins feel flat. The good days feel far away. And you start going through the motions of a life you can't quite feel yourself living.
This is far more common in guys your age than anyone admits. Half the men around you are quietly numb and calling it being fine.
Numbness isn't who you are. It's armor you put on for a reason that made sense once, and then forgot to take off.
You don't fix it by forcing some big feeling. You start small. One thing you used to love, done slowly, with your phone in the other room.
Feeling comes back the way a foot wakes up. Uncomfortable at first. Then alive.
You're not broken. You've just been bracing so long you forgot what it feels like to set it down.
