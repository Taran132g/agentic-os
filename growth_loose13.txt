There's a version of you that you can picture clearly. Disciplined. In shape. Actually building something. And every night you scroll past him a little longer.
You know the feeling. Putting the phone down at 2am with that low hum of... I wasted another one.
And the shame doesn't make you get up earlier. It just makes tomorrow feel heavier before it's even started.
So let me take one thing off your back. You're not lazy. You're overwhelmed and under-encouraged, trying to fix a motivation problem that was actually an exhaustion problem the whole time.
That guy you keep picturing isn't built in one heroic, all-or-nothing day. He's built in the boring five minutes you almost skipped.
And you're not behind because you lack potential. Half the guys who look like they've got it together are fighting the same 2am scroll you are.
You don't need to become him tonight. You need one rep. One page. One walk. One thing your future self can point back to and say, that's where it turned.
Small and unglamorous, done today, beats perfect and postponed.
Stop waiting to feel like him before you act like him. It has always worked the other way around.
