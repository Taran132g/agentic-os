There's a person you used to talk to every day. Now it's been months. Nothing happened. No fight, no falling out. You just... drifted.
And some nights you think about texting them. And then you don't. Because it's been too long, and reaching out now would feel weird, and what if they've moved on.
So you let another week pass. And the distance quietly becomes permanent.
Here's what nobody tells you about losing people. Most of the time it isn't a dramatic goodbye. It's a slow fade nobody chose, that everybody just let happen.
And the wild part is, they probably miss you the exact same way. Sitting on the exact same unsent text. Each of you assuming the other one stopped caring.
Growing apart isn't always proof it was meant to end. Sometimes it's just two people who got busy, and scared, and waited too long to reach.
You don't need a reason or a perfect message. You just send the thing. "Hey. I was thinking about you. Miss you."
Half the time, that one text is all it takes to close a distance that was never really about not caring.
Don't let someone become a stranger just because reaching out felt awkward for thirty seconds.
