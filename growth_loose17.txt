You've played a version of yourself for so long that you're not totally sure who's underneath anymore.
There's the you at work. The you with friends. The you your family gets. Each one edited, adjusted, performing. And somewhere in all of it, the real one went quiet.
Here's the part that scares you. Even alone, in your own room, the performance doesn't fully stop. You catch yourself narrating, managing, being watched by no one.
That's not you being fake. That's a kid who learned early that the real version wasn't safe to show, so he built one that was.
And it worked. It kept you liked. It kept you accepted. But it also means nobody has ever really met the person actually living your life.
You don't find yourself by thinking harder about who you are. You find him in the small unguarded moments you usually rush past.
What you laugh at when no one's around to judge it. What you actually think, before you edit it for the room.
That guy is still in there. He never left. He just got so used to being managed that he stopped speaking up.
You don't have to perform to be worth something. You just have to let yourself be, and find out you were enough underneath the act the whole time.
