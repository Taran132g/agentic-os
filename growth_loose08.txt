The kid who used to watch videos exactly like this one after school... has a question for you.
He'd drop his backpack by the door, grab a snack, and watch someone play a block game for hours. No deadlines. No image to manage. Just wonder.
That kid didn't dream about you being impressive.
He didn't care about the follower count, the internship, the car. He dreamed about being happy... and he assumed you'd have figured it out by now.
So here's his question. Would he thank you... for what you did with his time?
Not for the trophies. For the way you talk to yourself now. For how rarely you do anything just because it's fun. For how long it's been since you felt that after-school feeling.
Some of you would have to look him in the eye and explain why you gave up the things he loved... for things you don't even want.
You can't go back to being him. But you can stop becoming someone he'd be afraid of.
Do one thing today that eight-year-old you would high-five you for.
That's it. That's the whole assignment.
