There's a theory called the last meeting. It says you never know which time... is the final time.
Once two people have learned whatever they were supposed to learn from each other, life gets quiet about it. No warning. No goodbye scene.
You can live in the same town. Know the same people. Cross the same parking lots. And still never end up in the same room again.
I know, because it happened to my friend group. Every week last summer — same basement, same six people, same arguments over the same games.
One of those nights was the last one. And I couldn't tell you which. Nothing happened. Someone just... never set up the next one.
The group chat still exists. Same name. Same photo. Nobody's typed in it for months.
That's the part the theory gets right. Endings don't announce themselves. Most of them happen weeks before you notice — quietly, while you're assuming there's a next time.
If I'd known it was the last one, I would've stayed longer. Looked around the room a little more. Lost on purpose, maybe.
But that's not how last meetings work. You only find them looking backward.
Some people get a whole chapter... nobody gets every page.
