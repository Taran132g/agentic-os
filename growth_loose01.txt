You can laugh with someone through an entire chapter of your life. Every scene. Every inside joke. Every late night. And it still doesn't mean they're in the next chapter.
That's the hard thing about chapters. While you're living one, it feels like it will never end. You collect jokes, conversations, memories you quietly assume will follow you into every chapter after this one.
I've been watching it happen in my own life. My friend group is quietly coming apart. Not from drama. Not from some big falling out. From growth.
Some of us are changing. New goals, new habits, new versions of ourselves. And some of us are still the exact same person we were in high school. Same jokes. Same weekends. Seventeen at twenty.
And here's the part nobody says out loud. It's mutual. The ones who aren't growing are drifting away from me. And I'm drifting from them. We're all doing it to each other, gently, at the same time.
Nobody's the villain. Staying the same isn't a crime, and growing isn't a betrayal. The laughter in the old chapters was real, and it stays real.
Some people were never meant to be in every chapter. They were meant to make one of them unforgettable. That's a different kind of important.
The mistake is thinking every good chapter deserves a sequel. Sometimes the people who mattered most are the ones the story loses along the way.
Because not everyone is meant to be in every chapter. Some people are just meant to make the old ones worth rereading.
