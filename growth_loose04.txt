A while ago, I stopped texting first. Not as a test. Not to punish anyone. I was just tired.
Tired of being the one who plans it. The one who asks. The one who keeps the whole thing breathing.
I used to set up every game night. Every hangout. Every "you free this weekend?" — that was me.
So I put the phone down... and I waited.
Some people noticed. Most didn't. The plans just stopped existing. Turns out there were never any plans... only mine.
And here's the part that hurt. Nobody was being cruel. They were just comfortable. Comfortable that I'd always reach out first.
Effort you never see gets invisible fast. You only notice the bridge... when the person holding it lets go.
I'm not angry at anyone. But I stopped carrying what nobody helped me hold.
Text them first if they matter. But if you're the only one reaching... let the silence tell you what they wouldn't.
