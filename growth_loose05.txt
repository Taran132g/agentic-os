Nobody talks about the in-between. When you've outgrown your old people... but your new people haven't shown up yet.
You're not lonely because something's wrong with you. You're lonely because you're between chapters.
I know that space. The old group is drifting. The jokes don't land like they used to. And the people who get who I'm becoming... I mostly haven't met them.
Here's what nobody tells you. Your people are out there right now. Living their own lives. Feeling the same gap.
Some of the most important people in your life haven't met you yet. Your best friend at thirty... might be a stranger today.
So no — the loneliness isn't proof you don't belong anywhere. It's proof you're moving.
You don't find your people by shrinking back into who you were. You find them by becoming the person they'll recognize.
Keep going. Keep becoming. Somewhere out there, your people are doing the same thing... walking toward you without knowing it.
