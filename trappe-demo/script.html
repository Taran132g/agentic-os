Pitch Playbook — PAIS for Local Businesses

“I build little AI workers that plug the money leaks in your business — the regulars who drift off, and the phone calls you miss. You approve everything; nothing goes out without your okay.”

What to push (the template)

Run every pitch through the same five beats. Lead with the leak they feel, never with “AI.”

  1. Open on a leak, as a question. “The regulars who used to come in every week and then stopped — do you win them back, or do they just disappear?” / “On a packed Friday, what happens to the calls nobody can grab?”
  2. Lead with the two highest-ROI fixes: Reactivation + Missed-Call. These need nothing they don't already have.
  3. Show, don't tell. Turn the screen around. Two money-shots: the live missed call landing in the inbox, and flipping the AI Voice toggle so the call gets answered & booked, not just texted back.
  4. Upsell the voice agent only after they're nodding — it's the “never miss a call” upgrade, not the opener.
  5. Close on a risk-free pilot: free 30 days, then a flat monthly, month-to-month, cancel anytime.

Fill-in opener (swap per business): “You're getting [X] calls a month straight from Google — on a busy day some of those ring out. I make sure none of them do, and I bring back the [regulars/members/patients] who quietly stopped coming. Want to see it with [Business]'s name on it?”

On the phone — openers by vertical

Keep it to ~15 seconds: local + builder, the leak they feel, then the ask. Goal of the call is the meeting, not the explanation.

Self-intro (every call): “Hi [name], I'll be quick — I'm Taranveer, I run a small AI studio called Piontrix, I'm local. I'm setting up a few local spots with this, free for 30 days, and wanted to show you —” then the vertical hook:

  • Bar / restaurant: “On a packed Friday after the host goes home, who's catching the phone? Those are parties booking somewhere else. I catch them — can I show you for ten minutes?”
  • Dental / medical: “Every overdue recall is a cleaning you're not billing, and a missed front-desk call is a new patient gone. I bring both back automatically. Worth ten minutes?”
  • Salon / nails / barber: “A no-show is an hour you can't sell back, and the clients who never rebooked cost the most — I bring them back with a text from your own number. Ten minutes?”
  • Gym / studio: “The members who quietly stop showing a couple weeks before they cancel — I catch them and pull them back. Could I show you?”
  • Auto / service: “Every call you miss while you're under a car is a job going to the shop down the road — I catch them, and bring customers back when they're due. Ten minutes?”

No-pickup voicemail: “Hi [name], Taranveer with Piontrix — I'm local, I build AI that catches the calls and customers local [businesses] lose. Setting a few spots up free for 30 days. Quick callback? [number].”

In person — run the demo (run-of-show)

Before you walk in: demo loaded to their vertical, fullscreen, brightness up, Do Not Disturb on, charged. Refresh right before you hand it over so the live missed-call fires on cue (~9s).

  1. Hand it over (5s). “This is your morning screen — everything the agents did overnight. You just approve what you like.”
  2. Lead Reactivation (15s). Point at the top card. “These are [7] regulars who haven't been in 6 weeks — it already wrote each a personal note in your voice.” Let them tap Approve → watch the Recovered number tick up.
  3. Missed call (10s). “This caught a call at 9:52 last night after the host left — drafted the text back already.”
  4. 🎯 The live moment. ~9s in, a new missed call pops with a chime. “—and watch, that's a call ringing out right now. One tap, it goes from your phone.”
  5. 🎯 The voice upgrade. Flip the AI Voice toggle. “Or — instead of texting back after, the AI answers live and books it. The call never even rings out.”
  6. Gesture at the rest (10s). Wave at the agents, leak bars, daily brief. “Reviews, busiest hours, regulars — it's all here. But those two are where the money is.”
  7. The numbers. Open Google Insights. “These are your real Google numbers — [540] people called you from Google last month. How many rang out on a Friday? That's what we just fixed.”
  8. Close. “Free 30-day pilot, I set it all up, you watch the numbers. Then [$X]/mo, cancel anytime. Want me to set it up Thursday?”

Presenting tips: let them tap a button (ownership); point, tap, then shut up — let it react; don't tour every pixel; pre-set their vertical before you walk in.

The workflows (details to push)

  1. Reactivation — lead. Finds regulars who quietly stopped coming + first-timers who never returned, drafts a personal “come back, here's something on us.” Money you already earned, walking back in. Connects to: any customer list — reservation log, email list. No POS needed.
  2. Missed-Call Text-Back — lead. Phone rings out → instant drafted text back so the booking comes to them, not the next place. Sends from the owner's own phone, one tap. Connects to: the phone's “forward on no-answer” setting — a 5-minute carrier toggle.
  3. AI Voice Receptionist — the upgrade. The AI answers the phone live, takes the reservation, hands over a summary to confirm. The call is never missed. Connects to: a number we point their line to.
  4. Reputation — bonus. Drafts replies to every Google review in their voice + asks happy customers for new ones. Connects to: a 2-minute Google Business invite.
  5. Digest & Reminders — later. Morning sales read + booking confirmations; switch on when they add a POS or booking system.

Quick comebacks to hard questions

If they say…Hit back with…
“I don't have a POS / booking system.”“Three of these work with what you already have. The other two switch on later if you add one — I'd rather tell you that now than oversell you.”
“A robot answering my phone? People hate that.”“Your call. It only answers calls you'd have missed anyway — after hours, or when nobody can get to it. Right now those go nowhere. Keep it on for closed hours only, or skip it and use text-back.”
“Will it spam my customers / make me look bad?”“It never sends anything on its own. It writes, you approve, it goes from your number in your voice. You're always the last word.”
“Another monthly software bill?”“No bloated platform underneath, and your customer list stays on your side. You're paying me to run it — one won-back party a week covers it.”
“I'm too busy to learn software.”“You open one screen in the morning and tap approve. That's the whole job. I do all the setup.”
“How do I know it works?”“Free 30-day pilot. You'll see exactly how many regulars came back and calls it saved before you pay a cent.”
“What's it cost?”“Free 30 days, then [$400/mo], month-to-month, cancel anytime. If it's not earning its keep, fire it.”
“I already pay someone for marketing.”“This isn't marketing — it's plugging the leaks after they find you: the calls and the regulars you're already losing. It makes the marketing you pay for actually pay off.”
“Is my data safe?”“It runs on a setup in your own back office. Your customer list never leaves your side — only the messages you approve go out.”
“Let me think about it.”“Totally fair — that's what the free month is for. No card, no contract. Worst case you get a month of won-back regulars for nothing. Want me to set it up Thursday?”

Notes to self

• [$400/mo] is a placeholder — check 2–3 competitor offers before quoting.

• Open the Google insights tab in front of them — “you got 540 calls from Google last month” is a real number that sells the missed-call fix on its own.

• Rehearse the two demo money-shots: the live missed call, and the AI Voice toggle.

• The voice agent is the upgrade close — offer it after they're already sold on reactivation + missed-call.
