Somewhere growing up, you learned that most feelings weren't safe to show. Sadness got you called weak. Fear got you laughed at. So everything got funneled into the one emotion nobody could shame you for. Anger.
And now you're older, and that same anger makes you feel like the villain. You snap, you go cold, you say the thing... and afterward you're just sitting there wondering why you can't feel things like a normal person.
Here's the truth nobody handed you. Anger was never really your emotion. It was the bodyguard standing in front of all the ones you were never allowed to have.
Underneath the anger is almost always hurt. Or fear. Or a kid who just wanted someone to tell him it was okay to not be okay.
You're not broken, and you're not dangerous. You were handed one tool for a hundred different feelings and told to figure it out.
This isn't about never being angry again. It's about getting curious the next time it flares. Asking yourself, what's actually underneath this?
Nine times out of ten it isn't rage. It's "I felt disrespected." "I felt scared." "I felt alone."
Naming the real thing is how you stop being run by the bodyguard.
You were never the villain. You were just a kid who only got issued one way to feel, doing his best with it.
