So there I was. Twenty years old, watching my first sunset. And the last thing it taught me is the one I can't stop thinking about.
We go through life feeling like individuals. My grades. My money. My problems. But we're really more like waves. A wave rises, it moves, it crashes. It looks like its own thing. But it was never separate from the ocean.
You're one wave in an ocean of everyone who ever lived. Your parents. Your friends. Even the strangers who built the world you woke up in this morning.
So do one thing for me this week. Go watch a sunset. No phone. Just you, being a person on earth. And while you're here, put something back in the stream of human consciousness.
