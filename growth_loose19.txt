You feel a lot more for the people in your life than you ever actually tell them.
You'll think it clearly. He's a good friend. I'm proud of her. I don't know what I'd do without them. And then you'll say... nothing. Maybe a joke. Maybe a nod.
Because saying it out loud feels exposed. Vulnerable. Like handing someone something they could just drop.
Somewhere you learned that affection is risky. That if you show how much someone means to you, you've handed them power, and they might not say it back.
So you keep the warmth in your head where it's safe. And the people you love spend their lives half-guessing whether they matter to you.
Here's the thing. Almost nobody regrets telling someone they loved them. The regret is always the other way. The things left unsaid. The text you meant to send.
You don't have to make it a big speech. "I'm really glad you're in my life." "That meant a lot to me." "I'm proud of you."
It feels like a risk. It lands like a gift.
Say it while they can still hear you. The people you love shouldn't have to guess.
