I was one of seventy-two thousand people at Wembley Stadium the day Freddie Mercury held the entire planet in his hand.
July 13th, 1985.
Live Aid.
We'd been standing for eight hours through nineteen acts.
Then four men walked out, and Freddie sat down at the piano.
The first notes of Bohemian Rhapsody hit, and the stadium moved like one body.
He did that ay-oh echo, and seventy-two thousand strangers answered him in perfect unison.
During Radio Ga Ga, every single hand around me clapped in sync.
I looked up, and it was an ocean.
One point nine billion people were watching on TV.
Almost half the planet.
And he played those twenty minutes like he was singing to each of us alone.
I've been to hundreds of concerts since, and nothing has ever come close.
We weren't watching a show.
We were inside the greatest twenty minutes in music history.
