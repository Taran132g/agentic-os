There's a reason you say "I'm fine" before anyone's even asked. You decided a long time ago that your problems were yours to carry, and nobody else's to hold.
So when it gets heavy, you go quiet. You handle it alone at 2am. Because somewhere you learned that needing people makes you a burden.
Let me tell you where that actually comes from. It's not strength. It's a quiet fear that if you ever leaned, the person would buckle... or worse, leave.
So you became low-maintenance. Self-sufficient. The guy who never asks for anything. And you called it independence, but a lot of the time it's just loneliness with better branding.
Here's the part nobody says to young men. You are not too heavy. You were just never taught that the right people actually want to help carry it.
The ones who love you aren't keeping score of what you owe them. They're waiting for you to trust them enough to let them in.
You don't have to unload everything at once. You just have to let one person carry one thing with you. "Hey... can I talk to you about something."
That's not weakness. That's you finding out you were never as alone as you made yourself.
You were never a burden. You just got so used to holding it that you forgot you were allowed to set it down next to someone.
