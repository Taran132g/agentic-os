This week I'm packing up my first apartment... and it's so much quieter than I remember.
When I lived there, I complained about the noise. Doors slamming. Someone's music through the wall. People laughing in the kitchen at 2am when I had an 8am.
I used to close my door to get away from it.
Now the boxes are stacked, the walls are bare... and I'd give a lot to hear any of it again.
Here's what nobody tells you. The noise was never the problem. The noise was the proof... that people were close enough to bother you.
You don't get a warning before the last loud night. It just happens. And then one day you're standing in an empty room, holding the keys, realizing the silence you wanted... came with everyone gone.
One day, you'll miss the noise.
So tonight, if the house is loud... don't close your door.
That sound is what home was.
